Personalized Suggestions

{% if dmft_index >= 0 and dmft_index <= 5 %}

DMFT Index 0-5 (Low)

- Brush teeth twice daily with fluoride toothpaste and use interdental cleaning aids like dental floss or interdental brushes to prevent plaque accumulation (Frontiers)

- Limit the intake of sugary foods and drinks to prevent the development of cavities. Focus on a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and water.

- Visit the dentist at least once a year for routine check-ups and professional cleaning to maintain oral health (JAMA Network)

- If at a higher risk due to other factors (e.g., dry mouth), use fluoride mouthwash or get professional fluoride treatments during dental visits to strengthen enamel and prevent decay.

- Limit added sugar intake to 24g/day for adult women, 36g/day for adult men, and 24g/day or lower for children. Reducing sugar consumption can directly impact caries development. School-based programs have also shown effectiveness in maintaining a low DMFT index (BioMed Central) (JAMA Network)

- Regular fluoride varnish applications (every 3 to 6 months) strengthen teeth and prevent decay. Improved caries outcomes have been noted in high-risk children with this practice (JAMA Network [1]) (JAMA Network [2])

- Apply dental sealants to molars to prevent caries formation, especially in children and adolescents. Sealants create a physical barrier against food particles and bacteria, reducing the risk of cavities (BioMed Central)

- Teaching proper brushing and flossing techniques and encouraging regular dental check-ups can maintain oral health (MDPI)

{% elif dmft_index >= 6 and dmft_index <= 10 %}

DMFT Index 6-10 (Moderate)

- Schedule professional dental cleanings at least every six months to remove plaque and tartar build-up effectively.

- Consider applying dental sealants to the pits and fissures of molars, which are prone to decay, and use fluoride varnishes during dental appointments to enhance enamel resistance (Frontiers)

- Engage in more frequent oral health education, including guidance on proper brushing and flossing techniques to address any gaps in oral hygiene practices.

- If experiencing dry mouth, use saliva substitutes containing lactoperoxidase, lysozyme, glucose oxidase, and lactoferrin to help control oral inflammation and plaque formation (Frontiers)

- Schedule cleanings at least every six months to manage plaque and tartar effectively. Studies show that children with moderate DMFT scores benefit from regular cleanings and professional fluoride treatments (BioMed Central) (MDPI)

- Applying fluoride varnish more frequently (every 3 months) can provide ongoing enamel protection and reduce caries progression (JAMA Network [1]) (JAMA Network [2])

- Advise patients to follow a balanced diet low in sugary snacks and beverages. Reducing sugar intake is crucial, especially for adolescents who frequently consume sweets, to prevent caries (BioMed Central)

- Utilize ART and fissure sealants, which use materials like glass ionomer to re-mineralize tooth surfaces, helping to manage moderate caries (BioMed Central)

{% else %}

DMFT Index >10 (High)

- Visit the dentist every 3-4 months for thorough assessments and treatment of active decay.

- Address any decayed teeth with appropriate fillings or restorative procedures to halt further damage and tooth loss.

- Implement intensive fluoride therapy, such as prescription fluoride toothpaste or gel, to provide additional protection against cavities.

- Work with a dental professional to create a diet plan that minimizes sugary and acidic food and drink intake, which contributes to decay.

- For patients with conditions leading to dry mouth, regular use of saliva substitutes and sugar-free chewing gum can help maintain moisture and reduce the risk of cavities (Frontiers)

- Implement intensive fluoride therapy, such as topical fluoride gels or varnishes, applied every 3 to 4 months. This can help to arrest caries progression and strengthen enamel (JAMA Network [1]) (JAMA Network [2])

- Use a combination of preventive measures, including ART, fissure sealants, fluoride varnish applications, and dietary sugar reduction, to effectively manage high caries risk and prevent further tooth damage (BioMed Central)

- Provide counseling on proper oral care practices and healthy dietary habits. This supports long-term oral health improvement, especially for those requiring intensive management (JAMA Network)

- For those with limited access to dental care, remote dental assessments using teledentistry can monitor oral health, offer preventive advice, and facilitate timely interventions (PLOS)
